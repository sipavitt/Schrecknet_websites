Project HMT-04: Haemoglobin Microtesting Trial

Lead Researcher: Dr S. Patel

The HMT-04 project evaluates the accuracy and reliability of low-volume haemoglobin testing devices intended for point-of-care environments. The study compares microfluidic sampling reliability across different temperatures, storage conditions, and patient groups.

Phase II testing demonstrated strong inter-device correlation, with a variance margin of 1.4–1.7% across repeated tests. Phase III will assess longer-term device stability over a nine-month period.
